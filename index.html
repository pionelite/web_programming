Mr. Crack from Indonesia

Prof. Dr. Ing. H. Bacharuddin Jusuf Habibie, FREng, aircraft engineer and third president of indonesia

Here’s time line of BJ Habibie’s life:

  • 1936 Born in Parepare, south Sulawesi
  • 1954 Studied mechanical engineering at the Faculty of Engineering, University of Indonesia Bandung (now the Bandung Institute of Technology)
  • 1955 Continued his study at the Technische Hochschule Aachen (RWTH Aachen University) in Aachen, Germany
  • 1960 Received an engineer's degree in Germany with the title Diplom-Ingenieur. He remained in Germany as a research assistant under Hans Ebner at the Lehrstuhl und Institut für Leichtbau, RWTH Aachen to conduct research for his doctoral degree
  • 1962 Married to Hasri Ainun Besari, a medical doctor. The couple had two sons, Ilham Akbar Habibie and Thareq Kemal Habibie
  • 1965 Habibie delivered his dissertation in aerospace engineering and received the grade of "very good," giving him the title Doktoringenieur (Dr.-Ing. after finishing college Habibie did accept a position with Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm (MBB) in Hamburg.
  • 1969 Developed theories on thermodynamics, construction, and aerodynamics known as the Habibie Factor, Habibie Theorem, and Habibie Method when He worked for Messerschmitt-Bölkow-Blohm on the development of the Airbus A-300B aircraft.
  • 1974 President Suharto recruited Habibie to return to Indonesia as part of his drive to industrialize and develop the country
  • 1978 Habibie was appointed as State Minister of Research and Technology. Habibie was, continuously, a member of six Indonesian cabinets for over 20 years
  • 1986 Habibie as CEO of Indonesian Aerospace designed the N250 Gatotkaca aircraft and was first tested in 1955, but unfortunately this project was stopped due to the crisis
  • 1992 Receives Theodore Von Karman Award
  • 1994 Receives Edwar Warner Award
  • 1998 Just two months into Habibie's vice presidency, Suharto announced his resignation. Habibie, as the Constitution says, succeeded him as president.
  • 2010 Honored with an Honorary PhD degree in Technology by the University of Indonesia for his contribution to science in practice as a technocrat
  • 2019 Dies at the age of 83
